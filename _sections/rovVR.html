ROV VR was a collaborative project between UC Santa Cruz, the Monterey Bay Area Research Institute (MBARI) and the Olin School of Engineering. I worked with various researchers across these institutions in evaluating the usability of a VR application to help pilots operate underwater remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) for scientific marine research.

Our focus was on evaluating the differences in usability, cognitive load and task performance when comparing existing ROV teleoperation formats with 2D monitors to a monosocpic and stereoscopic ROV VR teleoperation interface. More details on our work can be found in our recent publication to VRST 2021.
