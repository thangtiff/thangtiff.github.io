Role:

UX Researcher

Problem:

  • The COVID-19 pandemic caused many in-person mental health and psychological support services for individuals with developmental disabilities to rapidly shift to online telehealth platforms to provide services to clients remotely

  • With little time to translate in-person exercises to be effective for virtual sessions, it has yet to be understood what the impact of this transition was like for both practitioners and their clients

Solution:


  • A study was conducted with psychologists, support staff and clients at Hope Services to understand their experiences transitioning from in-person services to telehealth

Methods:


  • Conducted in-depth interviews with 10 psychologists and support staff regarding their experience using telehealth during the pandemic and translating in-person exercises and treatments into a virtual format

  • Interviewed 4 clients receiving telehealth services from the organization to measure their experiences with the transition from in-person to virtual support

  • Utilized thematic analysis to identify common themes within the interview data to determine what factors of telehealth were beneficial and what could be improved





Results and Takeaways:


  • Time psychologists and staff spend with clients increased after transitioning to telehealth, more than doubling the amount of time per session spent with a client

  • Technical issues, such as poor internet connectivity and lack of experience using telehealth programs, prevented clients from accessing services and increased feelings of stress

  • Findings from the study were presented and published in the conference proceedings of ASSETS 2021
