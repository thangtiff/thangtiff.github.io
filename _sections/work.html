Problem:

Orofacial cleft lip and/or palate (CLP) is a congenital condition that occurs when the lip and/or palate is not joined or completely formed before birth. This condition is often associated with a visible facial cleft, but also with psychosocial challenges, such as low self-esteem and social anxiety. Accessing a licensed psychologist for psychosocial support can be an obstacle, with 57% of US families with a child with CLP reporting that they travel over an hour away to their CLP care clinics .



Proposed Solution:

VR interventions have shown promise in promoting improvements in self-esteem and social anxiety for individuals with psychosocial disorders. Psychological methods proven to improve symptoms of psychosocial disorders, such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, are also easily translated into a VR format. In collaboration with global cleft organization, Smile Train, I designed and developed a serious VR game, Smile Proud, to increase access to psychosocial support from a licensed psychologist, and promote psychosocial development for preadolescents with CLP.

As the Lead Game Developer for this project, I ...

  • Managed a team of 14 people including 3D asset developers, programmers and UX researchers, and communicated progress to Smile Train stakeholders
  • Conducted user and expert interviews with psychologists and preadolescent patients with CLP to understand their psychosocial needs, analyzing data using thematic analysis
  • Led collaborative storyboarding and task analysis workshops with psychologists to design game content and UI
  • Designed a pre/post-test study to evaluate the impact of the VR game on the psychosocial skills of preadolescents with CLP in Colombia, Brazil, Mexico and the Philippines
  • Developed the game for the Meta Quest 2 using Unity, implementing multiplayer capabilities using Photon PUN 2

Achievements and Work in Progress ...


  • Wrapped up pilot user testing in Colombia with 6 preadolescent patients and their psychologist
  • Distributed the game across Smile Train's partnering hospitals and clinics in Latin America
  • Began user testing with psychologists and preadolescent patients with CLP in Brazil, the Philippines and Mexico
  • Created a Psychologist User Guide to instruct psychologists on how to use the game with their patients
  • Submitted manuscripts on the process of designing, developing and user testing the game with psychologists and patients to IEEE and ACM conferences
