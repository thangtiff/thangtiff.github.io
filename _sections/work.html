Role:


Lead Researcher and Developer, Postdoctoral Researcher

Problem:


  • Orofacial cleft lip and/or palate (CLP) is often associated with a visible facial cleft, but also with psychosocial challenges, such as low self-esteem and social anxiety

  • Accessing a licensed psychologist for psychosocial support can be an obstacle, with 57% of US families with a child with CLP reporting that they travel over an hour away to their CLP care clinics

Solution:


  • VR interventions have shown promise in promoting improvements in self-esteem and social anxiety for individuals with psychosocial disorders

  • In collaboration with Smile Train, I designed and developed a serious VR game, Smile Proud, to increase access to psychosocial support from a licensed psychologist



Methods:


  • Remote semi-structured interviews conducted over Zoom with preadolescents with CLP and psychologists to identify user needs and project requirements

  • Collaborative journey mapping and storyboarding exercises conducted using Miro with psychologists to design game to be integrated into existing psychological practices

  • Usability and user studies conducted to evaluate the impact of the developed VR game on psychosocial development for preadolescents with CLP

  • Qualitative and quantitative data collected on user behaviors, qualitative data analyzed using inductive coding and quantiative data analyzed using SPSS

Results and Takeaways:


  • Published and presented manuscripts discussing findings and implications of this work at IEEE ICDH 2023 and CSUN 2024

  • Psychologists using the game felt that it improved communication with patients and kept them engaged during sessions

  • Future work regarding the use of VR as a means of remote telehealth should take into consideration external factors to use, such as internet connectivity

  • Smile Train is in the process of distributing Smile Proud through the Meta Quest App Lab to partnering hospitals and clinics around the globe
