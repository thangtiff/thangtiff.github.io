Over the past 6 years, I’ve conducted UX Research, and designed and developed serious VR games with UC Santa Cruz’s ASSIST Lab. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher leading the development and evaluation of VR games for psychosocial support with non-profit organizations for people with disabilities and special needs, including Smile Train, Hope Services and TERI. My work focuses on understanding the psychosocial needs of individuals with disabilities and special needs, and designing and developing innovative VR solutions to promote their psychological well-being and development.

My research interests include:

  • User Experience Design and Research
  • Human Computer Interaction
  • Virtual Reality (VR)
  • Assistive Technologies
  • Serious Games
  • Usability/Accessability
