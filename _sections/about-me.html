I am a Computational Media Ph.D Candidate at the University of California, Santa Cruz. My research focuses on developing virtual reality (VR) games that provide social-emotional and psychosocial support to individuals with special needs. I've developed VR games for adults with autism spectrum disorders (ASDs) to help with social-emotional skill training, and I am also currently working on developing a VR game to provide psychosocial support to preteens with Cleft Lip and/or Palate (CLP) in developing self-confidence skills.

To learn more about my research, click here to watch my 2018 Grad Slam presentation on EmotionVR, a detective themed VR game I developed to help adults with ASD with learning and practicing emotion recognition skills.
