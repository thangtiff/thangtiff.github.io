EmotionVR was a detective-themed VR game created to assist adults with developmental disabilities in understanding how to identify emotions through identifying facial expressions collectively. Users were taught to consider groupings of character's facial features when attempting to identify someone's emotion, and learned these skills through playing mini games where they searched for suspects with specific emotions.

I presented my work on EmotionVR at the UC Santa Cruz 2018 Grad Slam competition, where I discussed the game and its impact in a 3-minute presentation competition. I have also presented and published my work at the International Conference on Disability, Virtual Reality & Associated Technologies in 2018 (ICDVRAT 2018) and the International Conference on Smart Computing (SMARTCOMP 2018).
