Problem:

Individuals with developmental disabilities, such as autism spectrum disorder (ASD) may face challenges in identifying emotions through emotional cues, such as facial features, vocal intonations and body language. It is noted that in the realm of identifying emotion through facial features, individuals with ASD have a tendency to focus on one particular feature, such as the mouth or eyebrows. Being able to recognize emotions is a cruicial part of developing relationships with others, often playing a key role in maintaining friendships, romantic relationships and professional relationships.



Proposed Solution:

In collaboration with non-profit organization, Hope Services, I developed a detective-themed VR game, EmotionVR, to assist adults with developmental disabilities in understanding how to identify emotions through identifying facial expressions collectively. I worked with support staff and clients of Hope Services to design and develop the game using user-centered design methods, including user and expert interviews, usability testing of various VR headsets (HTC Vive, Samsung Gear VR, Oculus Go) and high-fidelity prototyping.

As the Lead Game Developer for this project, I ...

  • Worked with a small team of programmers and UX researchers in designing and developing the content and theme of EmotionVR
  • Designed and conducted usability studies to evaluate the usability of VR headsets for adults with developmental disabilities and the impact of the game on emotion recognition skills
  • Presented my work at the UC Santa Cruz 2018 Grad Slam competition, receiving 3rd place
  • Published study and findings at ICDVRAT 2018 and shared my work at IEEE SMARTCOMP 2018 through a PhD Forum
